My Story
Jack Clark

Jack Clark

Missionary · Freedom House International

Roots

I grew up in Northern Virginia in a town called Leesburg, just outside of D.C. I was blessed to be raised in a home that honored Christian values and always supported and provided for my necessities, yet I spent most of my younger years ignorant of the Gospel and my purpose in this life. My sister was diagnosed with Lyme disease when I was in Middle School and from then on life looked very different for my family. It was around this time that I became very independent and started to dream about what I wanted for my life. I had an interest in business and a love for sports, and I had heard from a young age that I was a natural leader, yet I never truly knew what that meant. I decided to thrust myself into basketball with the goal of playing in college. I spent most of my high school years studying, playing basketball, and running around getting into trouble with friends. Although I was not following Jesus at the time, I look back on my time with our FCA coach and can see the seeds that were being planted in my soul. I remember one practice he asked us to all pull out a piece of paper and write down our "why". Why were we here? Why were we on this team? I wrote on that paper "to be a part of something bigger than myself". There was something bigger than me.

The Search

My junior year Covid-19 hit and I suddenly saw my dreams of playing basketball at the next level fading away. I decided to attend Northeastern University in Boston to study Data Science and Finance. I went into college with my mind set on money and climbing the ladder of success. My first year I quickly found myself unsatisfied and lacking purpose. That summer I came back to Virginia and sat down with a family friend who began to share about his time in an elite military position during the 20 year war on terror. Those conversations sparked something within me — a purpose and a passion. I found myself reading every book I could find about the war on terror that summer. I had grown up hearing about 9/11, and I had close friends who were Muslim whom I loved deeply. As I began to see the magnitude of evil in that war and that region, I began to ask very deep questions. After that summer I decided I wanted to join the military — not because I believed it was the answer, but because it was the only route I saw to take action. I began training for graduation to enlist. Yet as time passed I found myself growing bitter and empty.

Hitting Empty

Come my junior year of college, I felt extremely lost. I couldn't relate to any of my peers in their interests and passions, most days I was filled with anger and frustration, and I was discouraged by the evil I saw all around me. I will never forget the weight that I felt during the first week of January, 2024. I was working a part time job, taking a full course load, holding a leadership position in a campus organization, training for a jiu jitsu competition, and filling out an application for a marine contract. By every measure I was succeeding. Yet I cried myself to sleep every night that week. I felt like a truck that had hit empty and was pressing the pedal to the metal. That Friday I fell to my knees and cried out to God. I hadn't prayed a genuine prayer in years, I didn't possess a bible, I wasn't attending church. I prayed a simple but desperate prayer of surrender: "God I have been building my own kingdom for far too long, I am done, tear down everything I have built and build your kingdom in my life." From that day on everything in my life completely changed. God gave me new eyes to see the world, and He began to humble and break the version of me that had been exalted for so long. I found myself going to sleep with a smile on my face and walking in deep joy and peace.

New Eyes

That summer I got invited to go on a mission trip to Africa with FHI through a friend named Andrew. I spent a month in Zambia and experienced the power of the Gospel in a way that shattered the boxes I had built for God. One day in particular stuck with me — I found myself standing in a prison cell with men who had largely been involved in violent crimes. Many were bitter, staring at the ground, confessing they were going to shed blood the minute they got out. The Holy Spirit spoke through me and shared the grace of God with them. By the time we walked out their countenance was completely different, and they were shouting praises to God and clapping. In that moment I determined that no work could compare to this. I proceeded to finish my degree and went on another mission trip with FHI. The Lord continued to tear down the things I was clinging to. He also began to reveal to me the calling of a disciple to go to the ends of the earth and make more disciples. The great commission.

The Calling Becomes Clear

It was around this time that I read Living Fearless by Jamie Winship — the story of a man used by God to make peace under the employment of the CIA, watching the power of God diffuse threats of terrorism throughout the Middle East for 10 years. As I read this book, something clicked. The purpose and calling I had felt to join the military was truly a calling to fight evil, yet the weapons of our warfare are not of the flesh, but are mighty in God for pulling down strongholds (2 Corinthians 10:4). Jesus is and always has been the only answer. My perspective was becoming increasingly clear, yet my steps still were not. One day Andrew called me and told me about a discipleship school that the ministry was running — a 9 month program centered on discipleship and equipping in ministry, followed by 3 months overseas leading short term missions. I said yes. This time was foundational to my walk with Jesus and ultimately my life. I learned how to walk in community, how to pray and lead by God's presence, how to search the scriptures, and most importantly, I learned to hear God's voice.

Saying Yes

At the end of my time at this school, I received an invitation to come on staff at FHI, with the goal of assisting with the ministry's Data Systems and helping establish new ministry hubs in the Middle East. I prayed, I sought counsel, I went to the scriptures, and everything I heard from the Lord was a resounding yes. I believe that God has brought me here for a purpose that is far greater than me. An eternal purpose. A kingdom purpose. I am overwhelmingly grateful for His love and His mercy to save me, much less call me and allow me to be used for His purpose. My deepest prayer is that I would know Him more, that those around me would know Him more, and that He would be glorified through my life.
